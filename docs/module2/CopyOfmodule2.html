UNIT 2 - WHONET SaTScan Analysis: Configuration, Analaysis, and Interpretation

OUTLOOK

Unit 2 enumerates the step-by -step procedure on running the SaTScan analysis. At the end of this unit, the participants shall be able to:

  • understand the significance of site-specific resistance profile configuration;

  • perform the SaTScan analysis using real surveillance data;

  • be familiar with the SaTScan output and be able to interpret the parameters.

LESSON PROPER

SaTScan™ is a free software that analyzes spatial, temporal and space-time data using the spatial, temporal, or space-time scan statistics. It is designed for any of the following interrelated purposes:

  • Perform geographical surveillance of disease, to detect spatial or space-time disease clusters, and to see if they are statistically significant.

  • Test whether a disease is randomly distributed over space, over time or over space and time.

  • Evaluate the statistical significance of disease cluster alarms.

  • Perform repeated time-periodic disease surveillance for early detection of disease outbreaks (e.g ARSRL WGS and SaTScan Enhanced Outbreak Investigation)

Laboratory Configuration

In the Philippines, the Antimicrobial Resistance Surveillance Program (ARSP) has been essential in tracking AMR trends for more than three decades. The program’s annual reports provided critical data that guide national policies to combat AMR, focusing on prevalent bacterial pathogens. Some variations in the antimicrobial panels tested across sentinel sites possibly due to differing circumstances of each laboratory may have an impact on the quality and robustness of the national AMR surveillance data likewise in the detection of isolate clusters that can signal an investigation for a potential outbreak, thus a site-specific resistance profile configuration is recommended to obtain a more complete and reliable cluster detection results. The work instruction below describes the step-by-step process of setting up the resistance profile configuration.

  2. DMU staff creates a folder of raw database file per month per laboratory/ sentinel site for the previous analysis years plus the most recent daily submission of the site.
